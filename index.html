Contributions

Gates has contributed many things to the computer science community including his biggest achievement: forming Microsoft and developing softwares for the first microcomputers. Here is a list of his achievements and inventions:

  • He wrote his first software program when he was 13

  • When he was in highschool, he helped create a group of programmers who computerized their school’s payroll system and founded a company called Traf-O-Data that sold traffic-counting systems to local governments

  • Along with his friend, Paul G. Allen, he developed a software for the first microcomputers in 1975 while a sophomore at Harvard university which soon after became the world's largest software business

  • He cofounded Microsoft alongside Paul Allen and left Harvard in his junior year

  • Microsoft's first product was BASIC software that ran on the Altair computer

  • Microsoft licensed an operating system called MS-DOS to International Business Machines Corporation (IBM) for the usage of their first microcomputer, the IBM PC (personal computer), released in 1981

  • Gates manipulated the bigger company (IBM) to depend on him for crucial software

  • Makers of IBM turned to Bill Gates for their basic software

  • Around the 1990s, Gates became known as the PC industry’s ultimate kingmaker

  • Beginning in 1995 and 1996, Gates refocused Microsoft on the development of consumer and enterprise software solutions for the Internet

  • He developed the Windows CE operating system platform for networking noncomputer devices including home televisions and personal digital assistants, created the Microsoft Network to compete with America Online and other Internet providers, and, through Gates’s company Corbis, acquired the huge Bettmann photo archives and other collections for use in electronic distribution

History

Bill Gates wasn't always a billionaire, he had to work very hard to get to where he is today. Here's a bit about his background and his life:

  • Gates was born on October 28, 1955, in Seattle, Washington

  • His full name is William Henry Gates III

  • He has an older sister, Kristianne, and a younger sister, Libby as well as his mom and dad, Mary Maxwell and William H. Gates Sr.

  • His mother was a teacher for a brief time and served on multiple corporate boards. She would often let Bill tag along with her when she volunteered in schools and at community organizations

  • As a child, Gates would read over reference books, such as the encyclopedia, for many hours and was doing well in school

  • When he was 13 years old, his parents enrolled him at Seattle's exclusive preparatory Lakeside School and he excelled in all his subjects, particularly in math and science

  • He spent most of his free time on a computer and wrote his first program, a tic-tac-toe program, in BASIC computer language that allowed users to play against the computer

  • In 1973, Gates graduated from Lakeside and scored 1590 out of 1600 on the college SAT test

  • He went to school at Harvard in the fall of 1973 for law school, but dropped out in 1975 to pursue his business, Microsoft with his friend from Lakeside highschool, Allen

  • Gates and Allen started to work together on their business and encountered some problems and disputes, but later their business, Microsoft, became the biggest software company in the world

  • He quickly became a multibillionaire and continuously finds ways to share his wealth

  • He married Melinda Gates in 1994 and they now have 3 kids ranging from 18-24 years old
